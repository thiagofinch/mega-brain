GO TO CLIENTACCELERATORS.COM/CALLBefore diving into the ads themselves, let's define what
these numbers actually mean - and why they matter.
What it means: How much you pay every time someone
clicks your ad.
Why it matters: Lower CPCs mean your ad creative is
highly relevant to your audience. Facebook rewards ads
that generate engagement by showing them to more people
for less money. A CPC under $5 is strong; under $2 is
exceptional.
What it means: The percentage of people who see your ad
and actually click on it.
Why it matters: CTR reveals how compelling your creative
is. A 2%+ CTR is solid; 4%+ is excellent. High CTRs indicate
strong pattern disruption, emotional resonance, or
curiosity-driven hooks that force attention.Understanding the Metrics
CPC (Cost Per Click)
CTR (Click-Through Rate)
GO TO CLIENTACCELERATORS.COM/CALLWhat it means: How much it costs to get one qualified lead,
booked call, or customer.
Why it matters: This is where the rubber meets the road.
You can have great CTR and low CPC, but if your CPA is
too high, you're still losing money. A CPA under $200 for
high-ticket offers is strong; under $100 for mid-ticket is
excellent.
What it means: For every $1 spent on ads, how many
dollars you make back. A 2x ROAS means you spent $1,000
and made $2,000.
Why it matters: ROAS is the ultimate profitability metric.
Anything above 2x is profitable for most businesses; 3x+ is
excellent; 5x+ is exceptional. The ads in this document
range from 1.8x to 6x ROAS.CPA
(Cost Per Acquisition / CostPer Call)
ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)
GO TO CLIENTACCELERATORS.COM/CALLWhat it means: The percentage of ad spend you recover
immediately from front-end sales. A 100% liquidation rate
means you break even on Day 1.
Why it matters: For low-ticket offers ($29-$97), liquidation
rate determines scalability. If you can recover 80%+ of ad
spend upfront, you can scale aggressively knowing your
backend will deliver profit. Several ads here achieve 100%+
liquidation.
